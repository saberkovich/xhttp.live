Next-Gen Transport Survives Advanced DPI Challenges

By Alice Packet, Senior Protocol Analyst

INTERNET ‚Äî XHTTP, a novel transport protocol built atop HTTP/2 and Xray Core, has emerged as the preferred circumvention solution after the fall of VLESS. Unlike its predecessor, XHTTP is designed to withstand even the most sophisticated Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) systems.

Introduced in late 2025, XHTTP integrates dynamic headers, adaptive padding, and traffic camouflage features. Security researchers highlight its resilience to timing analysis, entropy detection, and active probing.

"XHTTP represents a major step forward," states Dr. Ivan Proxyov, lead engineer at the Circumvention Research Lab. "Its design anticipates DPI evolution, providing both stealth and performance."

Technical Highlights

  • Adaptive Headers: Dynamic modifications to evade static signature detection
  • Multiplexed Streams: Efficiently shares a single connection for multiple clients
  • Encrypted Payload: AES-256-GCM / ChaCha20-Poly1305
  • Fallbacks: Automatic downgrade to conventional HTTPS if blocked
